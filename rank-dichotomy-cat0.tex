\section*{Setting}
Let $X$ be a finite-dimensional CAT(0) cube complex.
Let $\Gamma$ act properly, essentially, and cocompactly on $X$ by cubical isometries.
\section*{Definitions}
The rank of $X$ is the maximal $k$ such that $\mathbb{R}^k$ embeds isometrically in $X$.
An isometry $g\in\Gamma$ is rank-one if it is hyperbolic and no axis of $g$ bounds an isometrically embedded Euclidean half-flat.
\begin{theorem}[Rank Dichotomy]
Assume $X$ is finite-dimensional and irreducible.
Then exactly one of the following holds:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ contains an isometrically embedded Euclidean $2$-flat.
\item $\Gamma$ contains a rank-one isometry.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
